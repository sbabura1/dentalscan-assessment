I completed the full scan flow across all five views. This review covers task flow, on screen guidance, capture behaviour and mobile risk.

What works
    •	The flow is easy to learn. The product splits the task into clear stages (info first, images second).
    •	The five angle sequence lowers user effort. Users focus on one pose at a time.
    •	The live frame, step label and thumbnail strip support orientation and progress tracking.

What needs improvement
    •	Instruction design needs more focus. The key action text is too small and easy to miss. Put one clear instruction above the camera view in larger text. Example: “Turn slightly left and show teeth.”
    •	Quality feedback is too thin. The interface guides position but does not explain failure. Users need direct messages. Example:  “Too blurry”, “Move close”, “Open mouth wider”,  “Face outside frame”.
    •	Progress lacks validation. After capture, users do not know if the photo passed quality checks. Example: show status per image, accepted, retake needed.
    •	The disabled submit state needs context. Show a clear message. Example: “4 of 5 valid photos captured”.
    •	Retake should be faster. Each thumbnail should support tap to review and replace.

Mobile risks
    •	Upper and lower captures create the most strain. Users tilt the head, hold the phone steady and keep the mouth open at once. This increases blur and framing errors.
    •	Glasses glare, facial hair, low light, autofocus drift and browser differences all raise tracking risk.

Recommended changes
    •	Add live quality checks with plain language feedback.
    •	Add auto capture after a short stable hold.
    •	Show a short five pose tutorial before scan start.
    •	Save progress during capture and retry failed uploads.

Assessment
The flow is clear. The weak point is capture confidence. Stronger guidance and stronger quality validation would make the experience smoother, faster and easier to trust.
